---
title: "Workspace artifacts"
description: "List, filter, and publish or unpublish Tess Pages for your workspace from personal settings, with role-based access."
---

Workspace **artifacts** are the Tess Pages (published sites) tied to your workspace: each row is a hosted page with a subdomain, status, and link back to the chat that produced it when that information exists.

### **Where to find it**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Click your **profile** icon in the lower-left corner, then choose **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Artifacts">
    In the sidebar, select **Artifacts**. You will see the workspace subtitle explaining that you are managing published workspace artifacts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### **What you can do**

- **Browse** published pages for the current workspace with pagination.
- **Filter** by status: active, inactive, or all (when the filter is cleared).
- **Open** the public Tess Page URL for each row.
- **Jump to chat** when the source chat is known (`Open chat` / equivalent localized control).
- **Publish** or **unpublish** using the row actions. Unpublishing asks for confirmation because the public link stops working and the subdomain may be reused.

### **Who sees what**

Access is driven by workspace permissions on Tess Pages:

| Permission | Typical effect |
|------------|----------------|
| **Read** (`workspace:pages:read`) | You see **your** artifacts in the workspace. |
| **Write** (`workspace:pages:write`) | You can publish or unpublish pages you are allowed to change (combined with manage rules in the product). |
| **Manage** (`workspace:pages:manage`) | You see **everyone’s** artifacts in the workspace, optional **creator** column, and you can filter the list by **creator** (`user_id`). |

If you have **no** artifact permission but **can** manage workspace billing, the UI may surface an upgrade path instead of the table. If you lack both, you will see a clear **no permission** message.
